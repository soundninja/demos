Circus City Summer Show @ The Louisiana, Bristol, 01/08/15

The Circus City Summer Show promises headline slots from two of its most promising artists, support from a variety of others, and some very special surprise performances. On the one hand an EP launch party for Sam Eason, on the other a platform for emerging local talent; the gig is set over two stages, downstairs and in the main upstairs room.

For a small venue like The Louisiana to host a showcase like this is perfect. It’s great to see the bands and artists supporting one another and watching one another’s shows, and that intimacy and camaraderie certainly spills over into the audience. We can feel it too. If this is what it’s like to be part of Circus City Records, then where do we sign up? Seriously though, they are nurturing some pretty awesome talent.

Beginning with a spirited performance from Ben Cooling on the Bar Stage - a corner of the downstairs room, opposite the bar - he holds the attention of those who care to gather at the front. Meanwhile Dellavega open the main stage upstairs, with a thrust of high tempo, loud rock. The local four-piece heat up the room with Catfish and The Bottlemen-esque style. There’s even some moderate moshing from the Rod Stewart look-a-like at the front.

Singer songwriter Ry Curtis follows, with heartwarming tunes and a cheeky grin. His tracks are catchy, and it’s unfortunate that one of them, "Mystery Woman", conjures up memories of Peter Andre writhing around on the beach in his "Mysterious Girl" video. However, it does seem a few shrieking ladies in the audience wouldn’t be opposed to that from Ry.

Downstairs again, and 18-year-old Emba makes her debut headline slot. It’s difficult to hold a pub full of drinkers, but her pretty, high notes float all the way to the bar. Greater Bristol’s answer to Ed Sheeran, and not just because of the flame-haired hirsute similarities: what strikes you about main headline Sam Eason, is his modesty and honest appreciation for anyone who watches him. It’s the launch party for his EP With Hope, but he’s insistent that our focus is shared not only with other artists, but the record label too.

The jaunty stroll that is "I Would Love" demonstrates Sam’s sweet and unassuming lyrics at their best. The type of man who tells you, “I don’t look good in shining armour, but I’d fight a dragon for ya, I don’t know how but I could learn,” is someone every girl could be ultimately looking for. He’s an indie-folk preacher man with simple, touching melodies; and (perhaps most importantly) singing songs that are full of heart.

For a label based in the heart of Bristol, with a mission to “find the best in new music and bring it directly to your ears,” Circus City are doing a great job showcasing numerous acts to an audience who wouldn’t otherwise see them. For a wholly reasonable ticket price of £8, you couldn’t really ask for more.
