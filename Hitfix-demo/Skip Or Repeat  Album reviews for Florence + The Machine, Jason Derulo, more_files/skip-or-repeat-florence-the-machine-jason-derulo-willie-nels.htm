Repeat with some pause: Florence + the Machine, "How Big, How Blue, How Beautiful"

The intensity of Florence Welch's voice almost makes me wish this extra big/blue/beautiful album was also another B: bisected. After the bummer-pop's middle point -- which is the album's best, most realized track "Delilah" -- one should palate cleanse, maybe listen to some Philip Glass and work the Earth so something healthy grows from it. Because the sheer weight of this set may bring you down. The band covers Welch's metaphorical and literary bruises with thick instrumentation, wide reverb and poetic percussion, but when things threaten to sound downright chipper ("Hiding," "Caught"), Welch's spooky wail keeps this ship anchored.
