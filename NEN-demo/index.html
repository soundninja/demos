Festival No 6 Preview | 6 must-see upcoming bands at Festival No. 6

Kimberley-Marie Sklinar Green
September 2, 2015
97
SHARES
FacebookTwitter

Welsh weekender Festival No. 6 is almost here and we’re primed for another blissful few days by the Welsh coast.

Read our review of 2014’s Festival No. 6 here.

We’ve picked our favourite upcoming bands playing FN6 this weekend, so get a load of them in our Festival No. 6 preview before they’re headlining the event in coming years.

Stornoway

Stornoway are big fans of the outdoors, and make a perfect for the fresh and solace-filled location of Festival No. 6 – they recently toured RSPB nature reserves.

Hear this acoustic cover of Yazoo’s ‘The Only Way Is Up’:

The Orielles

Halifax trio The Orielles’ stoner/surf rock are taking their melodic show to Tim Peaks’ Diner this weekend – see them on the Friday.

Here’s the trippy video for ‘Space Doubt’:

Hooton Tennis Club

These guys and their grungy poppy guitar sounds just don’t stop. Hooton Tennis Club‘s debut album came out last week and they’re taking themselves all over the UK this October.

Hear ‘P.O.W.E.R.F.U.L. P.I.E.R.R.E’ here:

Seafret

Releasing a debut album later this year, Seafret are wrapping up a fantastic year of festivals with a penultimate show at Festival Number 6. Their enticing power pop-folk shouldn’t be missed.

See Seafret’s video for the heart-yearning latest single ‘Be There’, and be there to see them this weekend:

The Bohicas

Fresh from a stint at the Leeds and Reading festivals, The Bohicas are bringing their sharp rock and roll to Portmerion, with the sort of energy you’d expect from the likes of Bloc Party. Get ready.

Watch the video for latest release ‘Swarm’ here:

Cut Ribbons

Dreamy indie-pop is a great way to while away Festival No. 6 but when the band is Welsh too? Awesome. Festival No 6 has a real focus on local talent and Cut Ribbons are exactly that. Debut album ‘We Want To Watch Something We Loved Burn’ came out on 21 August so give it a listen, and we’ll see you at Village Green bandstand between 2-6pm.

See the clip for ‘Clouds’ from Cut Ribbons here:

We’ll be at FN6 this weekend right there with you. Follow our Facebook, Twitter and Instagram to stay in touch with NEN during Festival No. 6.
