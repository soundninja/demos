FEST 14 Preview: Kevin’s Seven

Kevin Rettie October 14, 2015 Columns, News Leave a comment 108 Views

Fest is one of the greatest times of the year. Friends past, present, and even future gather in one small town in Florida and party with some of the greatest bands on earth. But with so many bands playing, it’s damn near impossible to know who every band is. Fear not dear reader, Kevin is here to hold your hand and take you on a journey of discovery.

With big acts like Andrew W.K., Lagwagon, and The Menzingers, a lot of the littler bands can go unnoticed and ignored. Not on my watch. My goal here is to educate you on 7 bands you may or may not know about. Why 7? Mostly because it rhymes with my name and the internet is all about “cleverness.” Also because I wanted to cheat, and a top 5 just isn’t enough. Shit, a top 7 isn’t really enough, I could rattle off 37 bands you should see, but I won’t in the interest of keeping this “readable” and “engaging.”

7. San Diego bands: Western Settings, Caskitt, Sic Waiting

First one and I’m already cheating! I warned you. So, I might be biased being from San Diego and all, but the bands coming out of the part of the country rule. I have seen each of these bands play a thousand times and I am still excited to see them in Florida. Seriously, go listen to Western Setting’s Yes It Is and tell me it’s not an amazing album. I DARE you to not sing along to “Kicking and Screaming” or Caskitt’s “Friday Night Lightweights” from their LP This Machines Kills Sadness. I DARE you to not be impressed by Sic Waiting’s guitarist Anthony’s shredding or Jared’s between song drunken banter.

Caskitt plays at 8pm on 10/31 at Loosey’s
Sic Waiting plays at 9:40pm on 10/31 at Durty Nelly’s
Western Settings plays at 11:20pm on 10/31 at Rockey’s

6. North Southern California bands: Bad Cop/Bad Cop, Dudes Night, Gentlemen Prefer Blood

Again I’m cheating, but would you rather me group these bands into regions or use all 7 of my picks on Southern California bands like an asshole? So Gentlemen Prefer Blood and Dudes Night are both putting out/put out an album on La Escalera Records this year (a San Diego based record label! Weird). GPB released Used Books and Guns in April and Dudes Night will be releasing The Arsonist, The House and The Fire on November 14th. Bad Cop/Bad Cop may no longer be super under the radar being on Fat Wreck Chords and all, but they are one of my favorite bands and are just all around awesome people. So sue me.

Gentlemen Prefer Blood play at 12am on 10/30 at Loosey’s
Bad Cop/Bad Cop play at 2:50pm on 10/31 at Lot 10
Dudes Night play at 6:20pm on 10/31 at Loosey’s

5. Everybody Row

San Francisco’s Everybody Row makes me angry. “But Kevin, if they make you mad, why are you including them on your list?” Simmer down and let me finish. They make me mad because they are so fucking good, but they only have a 4 song EP out. So, it’s a good mad. They have an organ in the band, that dirty sounding bass tone that I absolutely love and dueling vocalists. It’s the perfect storm of music.

Everybody Row plays at 6:10pm on 10/31 at The Atlantic

4. City Mouse

City Mouse is one of those bands that just have a handful of EP’s and splits available, but have been around for a while. Fortunately they recently entered the recording studio to properly record a full length album. They also have the coolest shirt designs around, so if that’s not enough to get you to go watch them, I don’t know what is.

City Mouse plays at 8:10pm on 11/1 at Polomino

3. The Creeps

Last year my friend Lindsey was driving me around Oakland when this song came on in her car. She told me it was about a couple who had a suicide pact, but she backed out after he had already taken the pills. That band playing ended up being Canada’s The Creeps and their album, Eulogies, just might have been one of the best to come out in 2014. The Creeps also contain members of Crusades, so that’s cool.

The Creeps play at 1:00am on 10/31 at Rockey’s

2. Dead Bars

Speaking of Lindsey, she also introduced me to Dead Bars by describing them as a band with “a singer who can’t sing, and the lyrics are super dumb, but they are so good.” And she was right, they are so good. They also are a band who has a few EP’s and splits out, and recently recorded a full length album, due out on No Idea eventually. “Just Fine” off their split with Sunshine State might be the greatest song ever recorded.

Dead Bars play at 9:30pm on 10/30 at Loosey’s

1. Dollar Signs

I was perusing the Fest website one day, searching for bands playing that I might enjoy who are playing, when I came across Dollar Signs. It took every ounce of strength I had to not just skip them and move on to the next band. “There is no way I could ever enjoy a band with a stupid fucking name like Dollar Signs.” Well, turns out I was wrong. I hate the “this band sounds like this band” descriptions, but Dollar Signs sounds like Bomb the Music Industry fucked Andrew Jackson Jihad and had an unholy abomination that grew up on pizza and puking.

Dollar Sings play at 8:50pm on 10/30 at Durty Nelly’s

These are my 7 (11) bands I am excited to see at Fest this year. That’s not to say I’m not stoked for Off With Their Heads, PEARS, Direct Hit!, Devon Kay and the Solutions, War on Women, Banner Pilot, Two Cow Garage, Pup, Tim Barry, or the insanely popular Doors, but I said I’d keep this to 7(11) , and I’m a man of my word! See you all in a few weeks.
